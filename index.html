Research

Mechanical metamaterials, computational engineering, and machine learning.

Research project

My PhD research explores novel design pathways for damage-tolerant and ultralightweight lattice metamaterials for future air transportation.

  • Distorted and quasi-disordered lattice architectures
  • Adaptive sampling of finite-element simulations
  • Physics-informed and graph-oriented machine learning workflows
  • Multi-objective optimization of structural and material properties

Current methods

My workflow brings together structural mechanics, CAE tools, and data-centric modelling.

  • Abaqus, ANSYS, and simulation-led design studies
  • Python and MATLAB for modelling and analysis
  • Machine learning, surrogate modelling, and optimization
  • Applied mathematics including linear algebra and multivariable calculus
